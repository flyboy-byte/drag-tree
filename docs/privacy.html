DragTree — Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 3, 2026 · Last updated: May 3, 2026

DragTree (the “app”) is a drag-strip Christmas tree practice app for Android. This policy explains what data the app handles. The short version: nothing leaves your device.

Open source

DragTree is free and open source software, released under the MIT license. The complete source code is published at github.com/flyboy-byte/drag-tree. You can audit every line yourself to verify the claims in this policy. Issues and contributions are welcome via the repository.

Data we collect

None. DragTree does not collect, transmit, sell, share, or otherwise send any personal information, usage data, device identifiers, advertising identifiers, or analytics anywhere. The app has no user accounts and no servers it talks to.

Data stored on your device

  • Practice history. Your reaction-time results and chosen sensitivity setting are saved locally on your device using Android’s standard app storage so you can review past runs. This data never leaves your phone.
  • Sensor input. The app reads your phone’s accelerometer in real time to detect when you launch (move the phone). Sensor readings are processed on-device only and are not stored or transmitted. The HIGH_SAMPLING_RATE_SENSORS permission is requested only to allow faster reaction-time measurement.

Uninstalling the app deletes all locally stored data. You can also clear it manually in Android Settings → Apps → DragTree → Storage → Clear Data.

Permissions

  • android.permission.HIGH_SAMPLING_RATE_SENSORS — allows the accelerometer to be read at > 50 Hz, which the app uses to time your launch reaction more precisely. No data is recorded or transmitted.

Third parties

DragTree does not include third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or tracking SDKs. There are no third parties to share data with because the app does not collect data.

Children’s privacy

The app does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes in any meaningful way, the “Last updated” date above will change and the new version will be published at this same URL.
